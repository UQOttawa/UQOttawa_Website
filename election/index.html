VP Swag

Olivier Courchesne

Lors de mon passage comme chef, j'ai pu m'initier aux rudiments des jeux en plus de voir ce qu'impliquait la tâche de chef. Après une première année à apprendre des meilleurs, j'ai acquis des connaissances autant par rapport au fonctionnement général des jeux que dans la mise en marche d'une équipe fonctionnelle. Malgré mon intérêt plus poussé envers le poste de vp-swag étant donné que c'est la partie dans laquelle j'ai le plus aimé travailler lors de cette dernière année, il me fera plus que plaisir d'être chef ou tout autre poste au sein de la délégation afin d'apporter mes connaissances au groupe.

This last year as chief, I was able to get a grasp as to what the engineering games are and tasks the chief must perform. Learning from the best this year, I now understand how the competition takes place as well as how to have a functional team. Even though I am more interested in the vp-swag position as it's the part I liked doing the most this past year, I will be more than happy to be a chief or any other position within this delegation to bring my knowledge to the group.


Hunter Marriott

My name is Hunter Marriott. I am currently in my third year of Biomedical Mechanical Engineering at uOttawa, running for the positions of VP Swag and VP Logistics for the 2019 JDG UQOttawa Delegation Executive. I am running for this position mainly because of how much I enjoyed my experience at the 2018 Engineering Games. I want to be a part of the executive team to become more involved with JDG and help make every individual attending as part of the UQOttawa delegation have a great experience not only at the games, in but in the race leading up to the games as well.

I know a fun part leading up to the games was opening the swag bags to see all the apparel we got, like Christmas morning. I couldn’t help but notice while at the games some of the cool swag different schools had, and made a note of neat things UQOttawa could have next year for the games, for example, cozy sweatpants and winter jackets (if budget permits of course). I will do my best to make sure UQOttawa’s delegation has awesome apparel, while keeping costs to a minimum!

I think I would do well in the position of VP Swag as I have lots of experience in both ordering and designing items for different groups. I designed and ordered the patches for uOttawa Women in Science and Engineering (WISE) (go buy one at the ESS office for $5, they’re super cool!!), as well as designed and ordered the sweatshirts for the 2016-2017 executive team of WISE.

In addition to my clothing experience, I have a lot of experience in planning conferences similar to Engineering Games, but on a smaller scale. I sat on multiple conference planning committees while in high school, where the student committees planned crazy leadership conferences for all high school students in our province. I think this experience could benefit our executive team for UQOttawa JDG as I know the inner workings of events like JDG, helping planning for our team flow smoothly. The time I spent on these committees and the experience I gained will help me to be successful at either of the positions I am applying for!
